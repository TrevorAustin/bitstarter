Why Phone Press?

At Phone Press, we're concerned that ordinary people don't have enough say over what their elected representatives are up to, whether they're up in Washington or down in City Hall. And while the internet has made it easier to organize like-minded people, the kinds of actions that come naturally - likes, tweets, even email - are also the easiest to ignore. So we're building a tool inspired by the coordinating power of Kickstarter and Groupon to push them with the one thing they really listen to - the telephone.

How Does It Work?

An organizer with a cause picks a date that's coming up to run a Press - a calling campaign. Phone Press works with the organizer to set up an easy-to-share page to collect pledges to call and a threshold of callers and/or donations they want to clear. If enough people sign up, on the day of the Press they receive a notice reminding them of their pledge to call and a short reminder script. With any luck, we keep the phone ringing off the hook.

What's The Money For?

Mostly for web hosting for a trial period, though we'd also like to have a little to spend on a freelance designer and a little to register as a Not-for-Profit Corporation with the state and file with the IRS as a 527 organization. None of the money raised will be paid to Phone Press officers or contributors. More broadly, we want to make sure Phone Press is sustainable from the beginning on donations. It's very important to us that we be able to promise that Phone Press will never sell or trade your phone number or contact information to anyone, and making sure we're on sound financial footing from day one is an important part of that. Take a look at wireframe.phonepress.org to get a sense of what we're trying to build.

FAQ

What do you do with the phone numbers and e-mail addresses of people who pledge to call?
We will never sell, loan, or trade your phone number or e-mail address to anyone for any reason.
Can I use Phone Press to organize a campaign to call someone who isn't an elected official?
For now we'd like to stick to just office holders, though we hope down the line to expand to public figures generally. We probably need to talk to some lawyers about that.
